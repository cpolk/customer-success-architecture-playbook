# Operating Model

## Objective
A Customer Success Architecture organization should create consistent customer engagement without becoming a bottleneck. The operating model should balance strategic guidance, execution support, risk management, and internal coordination.

## Core responsibilities
- technical discovery and customer alignment
- architecture guidance and adoption planning
- enablement of customer technical teams
- escalation support for complex or high-risk issues
- progress reviews tied to measurable outcomes
- feedback loops to product, support, and engineering teams

## Engagement model
A practical engagement model often includes:

### 1. Strategic engagement
Used for executive alignment, major platform rollouts, expansion planning, and high-visibility accounts.

### 2. Programmatic engagement
Used for repeatable onboarding, standard adoption patterns, and maturity progression.

### 3. Escalation engagement
Used when customers face blockers, risk events, or urgent cross-functional coordination needs.

## Operating cadences
Examples of useful cadences include:
- weekly customer working sessions
- monthly success and value reviews
- quarterly architecture and maturity reviews
- internal risk and escalation reviews

## Cross-functional partnership
Customer Success Architecture is strongest when tightly connected to:
- customer success management
- support and incident response
- product management
- sales and account teams
- security and compliance partners
- platform or engineering teams

## Leadership expectations
Leaders in this function should create clarity, develop talent, improve consistency, and help teams scale from heroics to repeatable execution.
