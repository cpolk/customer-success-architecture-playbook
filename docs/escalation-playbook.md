# Escalation Playbook

## Purpose
Escalations are moments of truth. A strong escalation model protects customer trust, improves coordination, and helps teams move from reaction to resolution.

## Common escalation triggers
- production-impacting technical issues
- security or compliance concerns
- blockers during implementation
- executive visibility due to stalled progress
- unresolved cross-functional dependencies
- repeated incidents affecting confidence

## Escalation principles
- establish clear ownership early
- separate facts from assumptions
- keep communication concise and regular
- focus on impact, mitigation, and next steps
- document decisions and follow-through
- convert recurring issues into systemic improvements

## Escalation workflow

### 1. Assess
Define customer impact, business urgency, affected scope, and immediate risks.

### 2. Stabilize
Identify short-term mitigation steps to reduce customer pain while deeper work proceeds.

### 3. Coordinate
Assign accountable owners across the relevant teams and define communication cadence.

### 4. Communicate
Provide updates that explain current status, known impact, actions underway, and next expected milestone.

### 5. Resolve
Track resolution, validate customer acceptance, and document final outcome.

### 6. Improve
Conduct a follow-up review to identify what should change in process, product, enablement, or architecture guidance.

## Suggested update template
- **Issue:** clear summary of the problem
- **Impact:** who is affected and how
- **Mitigation:** what is being done now
- **Owners:** accountable leads
- **Next update:** specific checkpoint or time
