# Customer Maturity Model

## Purpose
A maturity model helps Customer Success Architecture teams assess where a customer is today and recommend the most useful next step.

## Level 1: Foundational
Characteristics:
- inconsistent repository practices
- limited workflow standardization
- manual delivery steps
- basic visibility into security controls

Recommended focus:
- establish baseline governance
- standardize pull request and branching practices
- introduce simple CI validation

## Level 2: Emerging
Characteristics:
- some teams using shared workflows
- early automation in place
- uneven adoption across the organization
- limited metrics discipline

Recommended focus:
- expand reference patterns
- improve onboarding and documentation
- define baseline success metrics
- strengthen dependency and security controls

## Level 3: Scaled
Characteristics:
- repeatable workflows used across many teams
- measurable delivery improvements
- stronger governance and policy adoption
- growing internal champions network

Recommended focus:
- optimize for consistency and maintainability
- improve KPI reporting and executive storytelling
- reduce exceptions and manual intervention

## Level 4: Optimized
Characteristics:
- broad adoption of standard patterns
- clear metrics tied to business goals
- mature governance with low friction
- strong collaboration across engineering, security, and leadership

Recommended focus:
- continuous improvement
- advanced optimization opportunities
- internal mentoring and change sustainability
